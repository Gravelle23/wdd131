Tips & Techniques

Learn how to crab like a local with these tried-and-true methods. Whether you’re new to crabbing or a seasoned pro, these tips can help you catch more crabs and enjoy the experience.

1. Choose the Right Bait

Chicken necks are a classic, but razor clams and fish heads can also attract crabs fast. Always secure your bait tightly so crabs can’t steal it!

2. Use a Proper Trap

Ring nets and box traps are common, but trotlines work well for larger hauls. Test different gear to see what works best for your location.

3. Go Early or Late

Crabs are more active in the early morning or evening. Avoid the heat of the day for better results.

4. Follow Local Laws

Make sure you know the size and catch limits in Maryland. Always measure your crabs before keeping them.

5. Handle with Care

Use tongs or gloves when handling live crabs to avoid being pinched. Respect the wildlife and release undersized crabs gently.
